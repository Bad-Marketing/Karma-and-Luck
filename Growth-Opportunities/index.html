Part One
Growth Opportunity Assessment
Opportunity Summary

Karma & Luck has established a strong Amazon presence supported by several high-performing collections and a diversified catalog. The review suggests there is meaningful opportunity for continued growth without requiring significant catalog expansion or changes to the current pricing strategy.

The greatest opportunity is to maximize the potential of the existing catalog. The review identified five Growth Opportunities that together create a connected strategy to strengthen customer acquisition, improve catalog discovery, reinforce perceived value and increase customer value.

Growth Opportunity 1: Protect Existing Revenue
Objective

Maintain availability across hero collections.

Observations
  • Historical stockouts impacted several hero collections, resulting in avoidable revenue leakage.
  • Current inventory health has improved significantly and is no longer a primary constraint to growth.
  • The opportunity has shifted from resolving inventory issues to protecting existing demand through proactive inventory planning.
Key Recommendations
  • Prioritize inventory planning across the brand's highest-performing collections.
  • Implement collection-level forecasting to better align replenishment with demand trends.
  • Establish regular inventory health reviews to identify potential risks before they impact sales.
Growth Opportunity 2: Expand Customer Acquisition
Objective

Continue expanding visibility beyond branded searches.

Observations
  • Branded searches continue to drive a significant share of customer acquisition.
  • Significant opportunity exists to expand visibility across Evil Eye, Red String and Crystal Trees.
  • Current PPC structure provides a strong foundation but remains primarily focused on individual products rather than collections.
Key Recommendations
  • Expand non-branded SEO across the highest-opportunity product categories.
  • Increase Sponsored Brands investment to strengthen collection-level visibility and New-to-Brand acquisition.
  • Develop a collection-based PPC strategy to support category ownership beyond individual ASINs.
  • Leverage Creator storytelling to introduce new customers to the brand through symbolism, craftsmanship and customer intentions rather than individual products.
Growth Opportunity 3: Improve Catalog Discovery & Customer Journey
Objective

Help customers discover more products by creating stronger connections between collections throughout the customer journey.

Observations
  • The Amazon Store is primarily organized around product categories rather than customer intent.
  • Collections are presented largely as independent shopping destinations.
  • Opportunities for customers to discover related collections throughout the shopping journey are limited.
Key Recommendations
  • Introduce intention-based Store navigation to better align with how customers shop.
  • Refresh the Brand Story to strengthen portfolio discovery and introduce complementary collections.
  • Create stronger connections between related collections (e.g. Protection → Crystal Trees, Bracelets → Necklaces).
  • Develop collection-focused store pages to improve product discovery.
Growth Opportunity 4: Strengthen Perceived Value
Objective

Reinforce Karma & Luck's premium positioning consistently throughout the customer journey.

Observations
  • Premium pricing is aligned with the brand positioning.
  • Voice of the Customer is generally healthy.
  • Opportunity lies in strengthening how craftsmanship, symbolism and gifting are communicated throughout the shopping experience.
Key Recommendations
  • Strengthen premium storytelling across the customer journey.
  • Complete the creative refresh to better communicate craftsmanship, symbolism and product quality.
  • Refine gifting positioning to strengthen emotional appeal and key purchasing occasions.
  • Create a more consistent premium experience across Listings, Store, A+ Content, Brand Story and Sponsored Brand creative.
Growth Opportunity 5: Increase Customer Value
Objective

Increase average order value through broader product adoption.

Observations
  • No bundles identified.
  • Complementary collections sold independently, limiting opportunities for cross-collection purchases.
  • Strong opportunity for intention-based positioning.
Key Recommendations
  • Develop a cross-sell framework across related collections (e.g. Protection → Crystal Trees, Bracelets → Necklaces).
  • Launch Virtual Bundles around key customer intentions and occasions.
  • Strengthen collection discovery throughout the customer journey across the Store, Brand Story, A+ Content and Sponsored Brands.
Conclusion

The review suggests that Karma & Luck has a strong foundation for continued growth on Amazon. Rather than relying on catalog expansion or pricing adjustments, the greatest opportunities lie in maximizing the potential of the existing catalog.

By strengthening customer acquisition, improving product discovery, reinforcing perceived value and increasing customer value, Karma & Luck can build a more diversified and scalable Amazon presence while reducing reliance on a small number of hero products.

Part Two
Strategic Roadmap
Customer Journey Framework

The objective of the Customer Journey Framework is to create a connected shopping experience that naturally guides customers through the Karma & Luck catalog while reinforcing the brand's premium positioning and increasing customer value.

The framework should guide the development of all customer-facing Amazon assets, including Listings, Image Stacks, Premium A+, Brand Story, Amazon Store, Sponsored Brands, Creator Connections and Bundles.

The customer journey is built around two core angles:

1. Discover Within the Collection

Customers entering a collection should first be encouraged to discover additional products within that collection, e.g.:

  • Protection Bracelet → Protection Necklace → Additional Protection Products
2. Discover Between Collections

Once customers have explored the collection, they should naturally be introduced to the next complementary collection, e.g.:

  • Protection Collection → Crystal Trees → Home Décor Collection
  • Protection Collection → Men's Collection
Framework Should Be Reflected Across
  • Listings
  • Image Stacks
  • Video Assets
  • Premium A+
  • Brand Story
  • Amazon Store
  • Sponsored Brands
  • Creator Connections
  • Bundles
Advertising Direction

How paid advertising supports the growth strategy · July–December 2026

Advertising's job over the rest of the year is simple: turn Karma & Luck's catalog into a connected shopping experience, bring the right shoppers in, and keep them moving from one collection to the next, so a single bracelet becomes the start of a relationship with the brand. This direction sits alongside the wider growth strategy and moves in step with it.

Our Approach
  • Lead with the brand, not just the product. Instead of advertising products one at a time, we'll advertise collections — Protection, Crystal Trees, Men's, Home — and lead shoppers into the store and brand experience. This is already the most efficient way the account performs, and it's how we build a collector's journey rather than a one-time sale.
  • Focus, don't spread. We'll concentrate investment behind the hero products and collections that matter most, at the accessible price point that fits Amazon shoppers. Deep and intentional beats wide and thin.
  • Attract the right shopper. We're not chasing the cheapest, lowest-intent clicks. We're bidding for shoppers who value craftsmanship, symbolism, and meaning — the ones who come back for the next piece — while keeping a close eye on profitability throughout.
  • Build toward Q4. Everything ramps toward the season. We'll grow the audience now, re-engage the shoppers we reached over Prime Day (which came in strongly, nearly doubling year on year), and have the store and advertising working together and ready to scale when demand peaks.

Inventory Planning Framework

This framework runs in July and August and continues to feed every downstream project — advertising doesn't get pointed at a collection until its inventory position is confirmed.

7.1 Q4 Inventory Planning Kickoff

First Week of August

  • Kick off Q4 inventory planning in the first week of August, ahead of the broader ramp-for-the-season phase.
  • Pull projected Q4 demand per hero collection and cross-check against current and incoming stock.
  • Set replenishment cutoff dates for Q4 based on supplier lead times, so POs land before the November–December peak.
  • Flag any ASIN that needs an early reorder to avoid a Q4 stock-out, with priority given to confirmed Parent ASINs (Section 6).
7.2 Replenish Out-of-Stock ASINs
  • Pull a current OOS / at-risk report across all four hero collections.
  • Confirm supplier lead times and issue POs for anything OOS or projected OOS before the Q4 ramp.
  • Flag any OOS ASIN that's also a Parent ASIN in Section 6 — these block that collection's PPC rollout and take priority.
7.3 Remove Aged Inventory
  • Pull the aged inventory report (181+ days) across the catalog.
  • Decide, SKU by SKU, whether to price-reduce and sell through or remove/liquidate — factoring in the aged inventory surcharge cost of holding versus the cost of removal.
  • Clear aged stock ahead of Q4 inbound shipments to free up capacity.
7.4 Secure Additional Amazon Storage Capacity
  • Review current IPI score and storage utilization limits.
  • Identify the actions (sell-through, removals, restock pacing) that improve IPI ahead of the Q4 storage request window.
  • Submit the storage capacity request in time to receive expanded limits before peak inbound shipments for November–December.
7.5 Advertising / Inventory Coordination
  • No PPC spend is directed at an ASIN that is OOS or within its projected OOS window.
  • Collection-Based PPC Rollout for a given collection does not go live until its confirmed Parent ASINs (Section 6) are in stock and the aged-inventory decision is resolved.
